# Lab Planning Notes

The first 4–6 weeks should use simple, safe, low-cost activities that build habits and confidence. Lab supplies are **to be determined** and may change based on student count, location, available equipment, cost, and parent feedback. The current supply ideas are a working list, not a final purchase order.

## Working Lab Model

The default plan is for labs and hands-on activities to happen during the Tuesday co-op meeting. Reading, vocabulary, worksheets, notebook work, and review should normally happen during the week at home.

Some activities may require a short at-home follow-up if results need time to develop. Examples could include checking osmosis results after several hours or observing a simple plant-related activity over several days. These should be occasional and clearly explained in the weekly packet.

## Suggested First Six Activities

1. Observation vs. inference activity using natural objects or classroom items.
2. Living, nonliving, and once-living sorting activity.
3. pH testing or food label chemistry activity.
4. Microscope observation of prepared slides or simple wet mounts.
5. Gummy bear, egg, or dialysis osmosis activity.
6. Catalase enzyme activity using potato or yeast and hydrogen peroxide.

## Possible Campus Lab Days

A campus microscope lab is recommended if offsite microscope access is limited. Other possible campus lab days include biomolecules or enzymes, DNA extraction and genetics, and ecology or classification.

## General Supply Categories

Supplies are flexible and will be finalized later. Current possible categories include:

- Printed worksheets and lab sheets
- Biology notebooks or binders
- Safety glasses
- Small cups or test tubes
- Droppers or pipettes
- Rulers and measuring tools
- pH paper
- Microscope and slides
- Household-safe lab materials
- Paper towels and cleanup supplies

## Planning Note

The supply list should remain flexible until the course size, meeting space, campus lab options, and parent expectations are confirmed.
