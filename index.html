Project PathBuilder · ARSG

Biology Foundations

A structured, homeschool-compatible biology course using printed weekly packets, free OpenStax readings, Tuesday co-op sessions, and hands-on labs.

What Is Project PathBuilder?

Project PathBuilder is an initiative under ARSG to support homeschool families with structured, academically grounded coursework. Biology Foundations is the first course offered through this initiative. It is designed for students in roughly 9th grade, with possible participation by advanced 8th graders.

The course is not AP Biology, dual-enrollment, or a fully accredited course. It is a well-organized, guided biology course that families can use as a meaningful part of their homeschool transcript.

What Is Biology Foundations?

Biology Foundations: Guided Study and Laboratory Exploration gives students a steady week-by-week structure for learning core biology concepts. Topics include the characteristics of life, cell structure and function, chemistry of life, membranes, enzymes, genetics, and more.

The course runs as a 15-week Tuesday co-op session. Students and families do most of the reading and worksheet work at home during the week. Tuesday meetings are for concept review, discussion, guided questions, and hands-on lab activities.

How the Weekly Rhythm Works

  1. At home (Mon–Mon): Student completes assigned OpenStax reading, vocabulary, guided questions, and worksheet. Parents supervise and check work at home.
  2. Tuesday session (75 min): Concept overview, student Q&A, discussion, and a hands-on lab or activity. The Tuesday meeting anchors the week but does not replace at-home study.
  3. Parent grading: Parents remain the primary graders unless a separate arrangement is made. Parent check guides will be distributed to enrolled families through a private channel, not posted publicly on this site.

Primary Textbook

The course uses OpenStax Concepts of Biology — a free, peer-reviewed college-level textbook written for introductory biology students.

Families are not required to purchase a paper copy. The full textbook is free online. Families who prefer print can order from OpenStax directly.

  • A parent check guide — distributed privately, not posted publicly

A Note About Parent Guides

Every student module has a corresponding parent check guide that includes a summary of what students are learning, an assignment overview, sample answers, grading guidance, and notes on common misconceptions.

Parent guides are not posted publicly on this site. They are distributed directly to enrolled families and co-op directors. This keeps student-facing materials clean and ensures families have the support they need without mixing answer keys into public resources.
