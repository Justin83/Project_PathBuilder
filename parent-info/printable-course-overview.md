# Biology Foundations: Course Overview for Families

## Course Purpose

Biology Foundations is a guided homeschool-compatible biology course for mostly ninth-grade students, with possible eighth-grade participation. The course is designed to give students a steady weekly structure for learning biology while keeping parents in their normal homeschool role.

The course will use **OpenStax Concepts of Biology** as the primary free textbook resource. Weekly packets, worksheets, lab sheets, and resource links will be created to make the course easier to follow.

## Weekly Pattern

Each week will follow a repeatable rhythm:

1. Students complete reading, vocabulary, worksheets, notebook work, and review at home during the week.
2. The Tuesday meeting is used for concept review, questions, discussion, and hands-on activities or labs.
3. Parents remain the primary graders unless a separate arrangement is made.
4. Answer keys or parent grading guides may be provided when helpful.

## What Tuesday Meetings Are For

Tuesday meetings are the anchor point for the course. They are intended for concept overview, student questions, guided review, discussion, lab activities, and hands-on practice. The goal is not to complete every part of the course during the Tuesday meeting, but to give students enough structure and support to continue learning during the week.

## First 4–6 Week Direction

The first several weeks are designed to build biology habits before moving into heavier content. Students will practice careful reading, following directions, using a notebook, learning vocabulary, making observations, and connecting textbook ideas to hands-on activities.

| Week | Topic | Main Idea | Likely Tuesday Activity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Biology, Observation, and Course Habits | Students learn how the course works and how to record careful observations. | Observation vs. inference activity |
| 2 | Characteristics of Life | Students learn what living things have in common. | Living, nonliving, and once-living sorting activity |
| 3 | Chemistry of Life | Students learn why water, pH, carbon, and biological molecules matter. | pH testing or food label activity |
| 4 | Cells and Microscopes | Students learn that cells are the basic unit of life. | Microscope demonstration or lab |
| 5 | Membranes, Diffusion, and Osmosis | Students learn how materials move into and out of cells. | Osmosis activity or demonstration |
| 6 | Enzymes and Life Processes | Students learn that enzymes help living things carry out chemical reactions. | Catalase enzyme activity |

## Labs and Supplies

The current lab list is a working plan, not a final supply list. Lab supplies are **to be determined** and may change based on student count, meeting location, available equipment, and parent feedback. The intent is for most labs and hands-on activities to happen on Tuesdays. If a particular activity needs time to develop, students may occasionally complete a short follow-up at home.

## Online Resources and Printed Packets

The course may use a combination of printed packets, online documents, OpenStax readings, YouTube videos, QR codes, and clickable links. Printed packets can support students who work better from paper, while online links and QR codes can make resources easier to access from phones or computers.

Parents make the final decision about whether linked videos or websites are appropriate for their student.
