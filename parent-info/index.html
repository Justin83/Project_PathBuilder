Course Package

What to Expect

Biology Foundations is a guided homeschool-compatible biology course built around weekly Tuesday meetings, printed course packets, online resources, and hands-on activities.

← Back to course home

Course Materials

The course will use both paper and online materials. Printed weekly packets will provide the main structure for students, while the online course page will provide links, readings, worksheets, resource pages, and updates.

Weekly Pattern

Most labs and hands-on activities will happen during the Tuesday meeting. Reading, vocabulary, worksheets, notebook work, and review will normally happen at home during the week.

A few activities may require a short at-home follow-up when results need time to develop, but the general plan is for wet labs and equipment-based activities to be handled during Tuesday sessions or occasional lab days.

Questions for Parents to Consider

  1. Does your student have reliable internet access and access to a laptop, Chromebook, tablet, or shared computer during the week?
  2. How many hours per week can your student reasonably spend on biology outside of the Tuesday meeting?
  3. Are you open to occasional lab days at the SCC campus for microscopes, equipment, or larger activities?
  4. Are there allergies, sensitivities, or safety concerns the instructor should know about?

Outside Resources

Some course materials may include links to YouTube videos, OpenStax readings, web articles, simulations, or other supplemental resources. These resources are offered in good faith to support the biology topic for the week.

Parents should decide whether each outside resource is appropriate for their own children.

QR codes may be included in printed packets for convenience, but phones are not required. Links will also be available through the online course page.
